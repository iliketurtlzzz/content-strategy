# MarketWake Editorial Guidelines

## Content Standards

### Writing Rules
1. **Lead with the insight** — First sentence should hook or teach, never introduce
2. **One idea per paragraph** — Dense paragraphs lose readers
3. **Use specific numbers** — "340% increase" beats "significant growth"
4. **Show, don't claim** — Results and examples over adjectives
5. **Active voice always** — "We drove 50 leads" not "50 leads were generated"
6. **No weasel words** — Cut "very", "really", "basically", "just", "actually"
7. **Short sentences win** — If a sentence has a comma, consider splitting it

### Formatting Standards
- **Headlines**: Benefit-driven or curiosity-driven. Front-load the keyword for SEO.
- **Subheadings**: Use H2/H3 to break content every 200-300 words
- **Paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max
- **Lists**: Use bullets for 3+ related items
- **Bold**: Key takeaways and important terms
- **Links**: Internal links to relevant MW services/blog posts; external links to authoritative sources

### SEO Requirements
- Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words, and meta description
- Secondary keywords distributed naturally throughout
- Meta descriptions: 150-160 characters, include keyword and CTA
- Image alt text: Descriptive, include keyword where natural
- Internal linking: Minimum 2-3 links to other MW pages per blog post
- URL slugs: Short, keyword-rich, hyphenated

### Blog Post Structure
```
Title (H1) — Keyword-optimized, benefit-driven
Meta Description — 150-160 chars with keyword + CTA
Introduction — Hook + promise (2-3 sentences, keyword in first 100 words)
Body Sections (H2s) — Each section delivers on the promise
  - Subsections (H3s) as needed
  - Data points, examples, or client results
Key Takeaway / Summary — What to remember
CTA — What to do next (contact MW, download resource, read related post)
```

### Social Media Formats

**LinkedIn:**
- Hook line (stop the scroll)
- 3-5 short paragraphs or bullet points
- Personal insight or hot take
- CTA or question to drive engagement
- 3-5 relevant hashtags
- Character limit: ~3,000 but aim for 500-1,200

**Instagram:**
- Caption: Hook + value + CTA
- Carousel: 5-10 slides, one idea per slide, large readable text
- Reels: Hook in first 3 seconds, teach one thing, CTA at end
- Hashtags: 5-15 relevant tags

**X/Twitter:**
- Thread format for educational content
- Single tweet for hot takes and links
- Keep under 280 chars for single tweets
- Thread: Hook tweet + 3-7 value tweets + CTA tweet

**Facebook:**
- Longer form OK (up to 500 words performs well)
- Question-driven to prompt comments
- Link posts for blog distribution

### Email Standards
- Subject line: 6-10 words, curiosity or benefit-driven
- Preview text: Complement (don't repeat) the subject line
- Body: One primary CTA per email
- Tone: Slightly more personal/conversational than blog
- Length: 150-300 words for newsletters, longer for nurture sequences

## Content Calendar Cadence
- Blog posts: 2-4 per month
- LinkedIn: 3-5 per week
- Instagram: 3-4 per week (mix of posts, stories, reels)
- Email newsletter: 1-2 per month
- Case studies: 1 per month (minimum)
- X/Twitter: Daily

## Review Process
1. Draft created (by this system)
2. Human review for accuracy, brand alignment, client sensitivity
3. SEO check (keywords, links, meta)
4. Final approval
5. Publish and distribute
